Books we recommend


The Bell Jar, sylvia plath

The Bell Jar details the life of Esther Greenwood, a college student who dreams of becoming a poet. She is selected for a month-long summer internship as a guest editor of Ladies’ Day magazine, but her time in New York City is unfulfilling as she struggles with issues of identity and societal norms. She meets two other interns who manifest contrasting views of femininity as well as Esther’s own internal conflicts: the rebellious and sexual Doreen and the wholesome and virginal Betsy.

The Secret History, Donna Tartt

Donna Tartt's first novel, The Secret History, contains elements of mystery and of the psychological fiction genre. It was published in 1992. The novel’s action takes place around the elite liberal arts campus of fictional Hampden College in Vermont, a the setting that mirrors Tartt’s experience in the 1980s at Bennington College, where she started writing The Secret History as a student. Set over a school year, the story follows a tightly knit group of six students studying ancient Greek who revel in the beauty of the ancient world but become tied together by a dark secret.

A little life, Hanya Yanagihara

A Little Life follows four college classmates – broke, adrift, and buoyed only by their friendship and ambition – as they move to New York in search of fame and fortune. While their relationships, which are tinged by addiction, success, and pride, deepen over the decades, the men are held together by their devotion to the brilliant, enigmatic Jude, a man scarred by an unspeakable childhood trauma.
